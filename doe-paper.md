This study explores the integration of novel perovskite structures into traditional silicon-based solar cells. We demonstrate that by layering these materials, thermal energy loss is significantly reduced. 

We propose a new manufacturing paradigm that allows for scaling these hybrid cells without the typical efficiency degradation observed in laboratory settings. Ultimately, this approach could yield a 25% increase in total energy conversion.
